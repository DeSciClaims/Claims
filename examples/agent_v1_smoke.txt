Synthetic Trial of Treatment A

Abstract: We tested whether Treatment A improves recovery time in a controlled
synthetic study. In a sample of 120 participants, Treatment A reduced median
recovery time from 10 days in the control group to 7 days in the treatment
group. The study used random assignment and measured recovery time over a
14-day follow-up period. The authors conclude that Treatment A improves recovery
speed under the tested conditions, while noting that long-term outcomes were not
measured.

Methods: Participants were randomly assigned to Treatment A or control. Recovery
time was measured as the first day on which symptoms were absent.

Results: Median recovery time was 7 days with Treatment A and 10 days with
control. No serious adverse events were observed during the 14-day follow-up.

Limitations: The study was synthetic and did not evaluate long-term outcomes.
